COMPRESSOR G - EQUIPMENT MANUAL
Model: CG-2000 Industrial
Equipment ID: E007

VIBRATION GUIDELINES
Normal vibration range: 0.5 to 3.0 mm/s
Warning threshold: 5.0 mm/s
Critical threshold: 7.0 mm/s
Compressors are highly sensitive to vibration. Any reading above 5.0 mm/s requires immediate inspection.
High vibration is the leading cause of compressor failure in manufacturing environments.
Recommended action: Inspect bearings, check mounting bolts, verify belt alignment.

TEMPERATURE GUIDELINES
Normal operating temperature: 40C to 70C
Warning threshold: 80C
If temperature exceeds warning threshold, check ventilation and cooling fins.

PRESSURE GUIDELINES
Normal discharge pressure: 70 to 95 PSI
Warning threshold: 100 PSI
Never operate above 120 PSI. Pressure relief valve activates at 125 PSI.

MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE
Daily: Check oil level, listen for unusual sounds
Weekly: Inspect drive belts, check inlet filter
Every 30 days: Oil change, bearing lubrication
Every 90 days: Full inspection by certified technician — CRITICAL for vibration issues

BEARING MAINTENANCE
Bearings are the most common failure point on CG-2000 compressors.
Signs of bearing failure: increased vibration, grinding noise, heat near bearing housing.
Bearing replacement interval: every 6 months under normal operation.
After bearing replacement, always recalibrate vibration baseline.
If vibration issue is unresolved after bearing replacement, inspect crankshaft alignment.

TROUBLESHOOTING
Problem: High vibration after bearing replacement
Cause: Crankshaft misalignment or imbalanced rotor
Fix: Check crankshaft runout, rebalance rotor, verify coupling alignment

Problem: Compressor not reaching rated pressure
Cause: Worn piston rings or valve leakage
Fix: Inspect and replace piston rings, test inlet and outlet valves

Problem: Oil in discharge air
Cause: Worn piston rings
Fix: Replace piston rings, check oil level
