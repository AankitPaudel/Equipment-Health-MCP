GENERAL EQUIPMENT SAFETY AND MAINTENANCE GUIDE
Manufacturing Floor - All Equipment

ANOMALY RESPONSE PROCEDURES
When any sensor reading exceeds warning threshold:
1. Log the anomaly immediately in the monitoring system
2. Notify shift supervisor within 5 minutes
3. Assess whether machine can continue operating safely
4. Schedule maintenance within 24 hours if reading is in warning range
5. Shut down immediately if reading reaches critical threshold

TEMPERATURE ANOMALIES
Temperature anomalies are the most common sensor alert on the manufacturing floor.
Common causes: coolant issues, bearing wear, blocked ventilation, excessive load.
Never ignore a temperature warning. Sustained high temperature accelerates bearing wear
and can cause permanent damage to precision components.

VIBRATION ANOMALIES
Vibration anomalies often indicate early-stage mechanical failure.
Catching vibration issues early prevents catastrophic failure.
A machine showing vibration above warning threshold should be inspected within 4 hours.
Document all vibration readings and trends for predictive maintenance analysis.

MAINTENANCE OVERDUE POLICY
Any equipment not inspected within 30 days is considered overdue.
Equipment overdue by more than 60 days must be inspected before next shift.
Equipment overdue by more than 90 days must be taken offline until inspected.
Maintenance records must be updated immediately after each service event.

SENSOR THRESHOLDS - ALL EQUIPMENT
Temperature warning: 80C
Temperature critical: 95C
Vibration warning: 5.0 mm/s
Vibration critical: 8.0 mm/s
Pressure warning: 100 PSI
Pressure critical: 120 PSI

PREDICTIVE MAINTENANCE
Use historical sensor data to identify trends before failures occur.
A gradual rise in temperature over 7 days indicates developing coolant issue.
A gradual rise in vibration over 14 days indicates bearing wear.
Flag any upward trend exceeding 10 percent over 7 days for preventive inspection.
